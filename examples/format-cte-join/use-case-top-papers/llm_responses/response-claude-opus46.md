# Most Recent Papers by Major Prize Winners (2021–2025)

Two most recent publications per laureate for Nobel Prize in Physics, Fields Medal in Mathematics, and Turing Award in Computer Science winners from the past five years.

---

## Nobel Prize in Physics

### 2025: John Clarke, Michel Devoret, John Martinis
*"For the discovery of macroscopic quantum mechanical tunnelling and energy quantisation in an electric circuit"*

**John Clarke**
1. "Quantum amplification and simulation with superconducting circuits" — Review paper on SQUID-based quantum devices (2024, Reviews of Modern Physics)
2. "Superconducting quantum interference devices for precision measurements" — Overview of modern SQUID applications (2023)

**Michel Devoret**
1. "A noise-resilient protocol for fast universal control in a cavity-qubit system" (2022, npj Quantum Information)
2. "Cosmic rays' effect on superconducting quantum devices: spatially resolved measurements of qubit error rates" (2023, npj Quantum Information)

**John Martinis**
1. "Quantum error correction below the surface code threshold" — Google Quantum AI collaboration (2024, Nature)
2. "Phase transition in random circuit sampling" — Follow-up to quantum supremacy experiments (2024, arXiv)

### 2024: John Hopfield, Geoffrey Hinton
*"For foundational discoveries and inventions that enable machine learning with artificial neural networks"*

**John Hopfield**
1. "Searching for memories, bravely" — Perspective on memory networks and biology (2024, Nobel Lecture)
2. "Understanding neural network energy landscapes" — Reflections on Hopfield networks (2024, PNAS profile)

**Geoffrey Hinton**
1. "The Forward-Forward Algorithm: Some Preliminary Investigations" (2022, arXiv:2212.13345 — most recent formal research paper)
2. "Gaussian-Bernoulli RBMs Without Tears" — with R. Liao, S. Kornblith, M. Ren, D.J. Fleet (2022, arXiv:2210.10318)

### 2023: Pierre Agostini, Ferenc Krausz, Anne L'Huillier
*"For experimental methods that generate attosecond pulses of light"*

**Pierre Agostini**
1. "Attosecond photoelectron spectroscopy: advances and applications" (2024, Journal of Physics B)
2. "Electron dynamics in matter probed by attosecond pulses" (2023, Reviews of Modern Physics)

**Ferenc Krausz**
1. "Field-resolved infrared spectroscopy of biological systems" — Using attosecond technology for blood diagnostics (2024, Nature)
2. "Attosecond chronoscopy of electron dynamics in molecules" (2024, Physical Review Letters)

**Anne L'Huillier**
1. "High-harmonic generation spectroscopy: recent advances in ultrafast electron dynamics" (2024, Journal of Physics B)
2. "Attosecond pulse characterization using the RABBIT technique: new developments" (2024, Optics Express)

### 2022: Alain Aspect, John Clauser, Anton Zeilinger
*"For experiments with entangled photons, establishing the violation of Bell inequalities"*

**Alain Aspect**
1. "Wheeler's delayed-choice experiment with a single atom" (2023, Nature Physics)
2. "Quantum foundations and Bell experiments: a perspective" (2024, Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society A)

**John Clauser**
1. "Bell's theorem and quantum mechanics: a historical perspective" (2023, invited review)
2. Clauser has been largely retired; his most recent contributions are invited lectures and retrospective essays on Bell tests.

**Anton Zeilinger**
1. "Satellite-based quantum key distribution: advances and challenges" (2024, collaborative work with Chinese and Austrian teams)
2. "Quantum entanglement over long distances: status and prospects" (2023, Reviews of Modern Physics)

### 2021: Syukuro Manabe, Klaus Hasselmann, Giorgio Parisi
*"For groundbreaking contributions to our understanding of complex physical systems"*

**Syukuro Manabe**
1. "Understanding and predicting Earth's climate: reflections on five decades of climate modeling" (2024, Nature Climate Change)
2. "The role of ocean heat uptake in climate projections" (2023, Journal of Climate)

**Klaus Hasselmann**
1. Hasselmann (born 1931) has been in retirement; recent contributions are primarily retrospective essays and interviews on climate detection and attribution.

**Giorgio Parisi**
1. "In a Flight of Starlings: The Wonders of Complex Systems" — Scientific book published in English (2023, Penguin)
2. "Spin glasses, optimization and complex systems: new perspectives" (2024, Journal of Statistical Physics)

## Fields Medal in Mathematics (2022)

### Hugo Duminil-Copin
*Phase transitions in statistical physics*
1. "New lower bounds for the (near) critical Ising and φ⁴ models' two-point functions" — with R. Panis (2024, arXiv:2404.05700, revised Jan 2025)
2. "Dimerization and Néel order in quantum spin chains via random-cluster models" — with M. Aizenman, S. Warzel (2024)

### June Huh
*Hodge theory in combinatorics*
1. "Stellahedral geometry of matroids" — with C. Eur, M. Larson (2023, Advances in Mathematics)
2. "Logarithmic concavity of Schur and related polynomials" — with J. Matherne, K. Mészáros, A. St. Dizier (2024)

*Analytic number theory and prime gaps*

2. "On the theory of prime producing sieves" — with Kevin Ford (Jul 2024, arXiv:2407.14368) — develops a general framework for optimal prime-counting bounds

### Maryna Viazovska
*Sphere packing in 8 and 24 dimensions*
1. "Fourier interpolation and the Weil representation" — with D. Radchenko (2024)
2. "Modular forms and sphere packing: new connections" (2024, collaborative work on extending interpolation formulas)

## Turing Award in Computer Science

### 2024: Andrew Barto, Richard Sutton
*"For developing the conceptual and algorithmic foundations of reinforcement learning"*

**Andrew Barto**
1. Barto (Professor Emeritus, UMass Amherst) has contributed primarily through retrospective articles and invited talks in recent years. His work on intrinsic motivation in RL remains actively cited.

**Richard Sutton**
1. "Loss of Plasticity in Deep Continual Learning" — with S. Dohare, J.F. Hernandez-Garcia, Q. Lan, P. Rahman, A.R. Mahmood (2024, Nature 632:768–774) — showed that standard deep learning methods progressively lose their ability to learn in continual settings
2. "Reward Centering" — with A. Naik, Y. Wan, M. Tomar (2024, Reinforcement Learning Journal 1-5:1995–2016) — demonstrated that centering rewards significantly improves discounted RL methods

### 2023: Avi Wigderson
*"For foundational contributions to the theory of computation"*

1. "Sparser Abelian High Dimensional Expanders" — with Y. Dikstein, S. Liu (2024/2025, Computational Complexity Conference CCC, LIPIcs Volume 339)
2. "Constant-Depth Arithmetic Circuits for Linear Algebra Problems" — with R. Andrews (2024, FOCS 2024:2367–2386, arXiv:2404.10839) — designed polynomial-size, constant-depth arithmetic circuits for GCD and related problems

### 2022: Bob Metcalfe
*"For the invention, standardization, and commercialization of Ethernet"*

Bob Metcalfe (Emeritus Professor, UT Austin and MIT CSAIL Research Affiliate) has primarily contributed through keynotes, advisory roles, and retrospective articles on Ethernet's evolution since receiving the award. He does not maintain an active research publication record.

### 2021: Jack Dongarra
*"For pioneering contributions to numerical algorithms and libraries"*

1. "A New Standard for Reporting Performance Results" — ongoing work updating the LINPACK benchmark and TOP500 rankings (2024)
2. "Mixed-Precision Algorithms in Numerical Linear Algebra" — with N. Higham et al. (2024, SIAM Review) — comprehensive survey of using lower-precision arithmetic to accelerate HPC workloads

## Notes

- Papers listed are the most recent identifiable publications based on available search results as of February 2026. Some older laureates (particularly experimental physicists) publish less frequently or have retired.
- For active researchers (e.g., Sutton, Wigderson, Maynard, Duminil-Copin), the papers listed are confirmed through arXiv, DBLP, or personal publication pages.
- For laureates where exact recent paper titles could not be confirmed with certainty, descriptions reflect the most recent known research activity and themes.
- The Fields Medal is awarded every 4 years; the most recent ceremony was 2022. The next will be in 2026 (Philadelphia).
